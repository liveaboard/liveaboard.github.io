How to clean the hull of your sailboat

October 29, 2020

In this post, we'll look into how you can clean the hull of your sailboat. There's a few ways you can do this, hire a diver, haul your sailboat out, or do it yourself! In this post, we'll focus on how to clean the hull of your sailboat yourself.

Before we dive in, an easy way to help prevent growth from building up on your hull is by keeping your sailboat moving! The more you take your sailboat out, the cleaner your hull will be.

Safety

Your health

Make sure to flush your ears with hydrogen peroxide after each time you dive, this can prevent swimmer's ear. Also make sure to be careful around certain dangerous types of growth like barnacles and oysters, which can harbor bacteria.

The sailboat and surroundings

Be careful of electrocution if you are planning to dive into fresh water that is near a shore power source.

Make sure to identify a way to get back into your sailboat before you hop in the water.

Never get in between your sailboat and the dock, or you could get crushed!

What you'll need

  • Diving mask
  • Fins
  • Gloves
  • Scraper
  • Screwdriver
  • Snorkel

Cleaning

Now it's time to hop in the water and begin cleaning the hull. Start scraping off any growth on the hull.

Instruments

Pay attention to instruments like the depth finder, make sure they are free of growth. Make sure to be delicate when cleaning these.

Thru-hulls

To clean the thru-hulls, use the screwdriver and stick it through the thru-hull, knocking off any growth.

After cleaning

Make sure to rinse your tools.
